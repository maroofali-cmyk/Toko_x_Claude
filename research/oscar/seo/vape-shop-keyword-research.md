## Research Methodology Notes

This analysis is built on the following strategic logic:

- Pakistan's vape market is growing but remains semi-regulated, creating both opportunity and sensitivity in search behavior
- Oscar POS competes primarily on price, FBR compliance, local support, and offline functionality — these are exploitable differentiators
- International competitors (KORONA, Lightspeed, Paradise POS) dominate English-language search globally but have zero Pakistan-specific relevance, creating a significant gap
- Local competitors (pointofsale.pk, technosysint.com, solutiondots.com) rank for generic POS terms but have weak vape-specific content
- Primary hijacking opportunity: capture all generic "vape shop POS Pakistan" variations before international competitors build local presence

- **"vape shop POS"** and all vape-specific variants are exclusively assigned to `/solutions/vape-shops`
- **Generic POS terms** ("POS system Pakistan", "retail POS software") stay on homepage or generic solutions landing page
- **"smoke shop POS"** should redirect or canonicalize to `/solutions/vape-shops` — do not create a separate smoke shop page
- **"FBR compliant POS"** is a shared attribute across multiple solution pages (restaurants, retail, vape); use it with the vape-specific modifier (`FBR compliant vape shop POS`) to avoid internal competition
